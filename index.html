This is what I do But truly, what do any of us actually ‘do’?

Yes, it's true, I design and write code. But I also pace the grocery stole aisles, searching for that peculiar item that once appeared in a dream. As far as I can tell, that unfamliar product does not, and probably never will, exist. It's not in the diary or produce sections, nor is it among the bologna or quinoa flour. Once in a while, I feel my vertebrae collapse like a fallen pyre. Before the paralysis stains me like an ink droplet in water, I stretch myself, my spine an extension of the celestial golden thread, to my full height of 130 feet. Or is it 130 meters? 130 some units of measurement. Anyway, what was I talking about? Oh yeah, ants. I stand eagerly, quietly, in front of my kitchen sink waiting for the tiny workers to emerge. Their arrival is as inevitable and enduring as death. I'm armed with a sponge, a vacuum cleaner, and a mortar and pestle.
